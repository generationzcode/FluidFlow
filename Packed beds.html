Packed beds

  • Used to increase the contact area between two phases
  • To increase retention time to ensure reaction completion (more so if the packing is a catalyst)
  • to have a plug flow in the reactor
  • In the true sense of laminar flow, a packed bed can never have laminar flow, since it's constantly being disturbed. It is still in laminar flow because of the friction factor curve
  • packed beds can also be used in drying by passing dry air up wet packing
