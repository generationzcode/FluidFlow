Problems

  • Many times we have a mixture where the two components are very differing in BP, typically in the petrochemical industry. So you need to separate a low BP, highly volatile component from one with a higher BP, higher molecular mass. If we assume ideal mixture, then relative volatility is the ratio of p1sat to p2sat. So if your boiling points of two components are close to each other, then relative volatility will be small. Also relative volatility cant be less than 1, because p1 will always be the more volatile component.
