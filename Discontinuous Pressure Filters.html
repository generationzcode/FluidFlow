Discontinuous Pressure Filters

  • These apply a large pressure differential across the septum
  • Economically rapid filtration for viscous fluids or fine solids

Filter press

  • has a bunch of plates pressed together tightly and liquid is passed through them and collected on the other side of each plate
  • the plates then have a solid cake formed inside them and when it's full of it and no more liquid can pass through, it's said to be "jammed"
  • the filter then must be washed and the cake removed, this can be done using a wash liquid or (maybe...) shaking it about
  • wash liquid causes a bunch of other issues since the liquid only takes the path of least resistance and the places where there is actual blockage to be removed by the wash cannot be removed easily.

Shell and Leaf Filters

  • filter press but contained in a shell and higher pressure
  • discharge manifold contained is from the shell and the excess liquid flows out of there
